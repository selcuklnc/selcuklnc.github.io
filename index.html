About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Texas A&M University working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, educational technology, and learning sciences. Drawing on a background in software engineering (MS) and science education (PhD), I design and study AI-driven learning systems, IoT-enabled sensor networks, and adaptive tutoring environments that support meaningful and equitable learning.

My scholarship appears in high-impact venues including Computers & Education, Teaching and Teacher Education, and proceedings from AERA, ISLS, and ACM conferences. I use mixed-methods and design-based research to examine issues such as ethical AI assessment, AI-TPACK development, computational thinking, and technology integration in diverse K‚Äì12 and higher education contexts.

Much of my recent work focuses on rural and agricultural STEM (Ag-STEM) education, where I co-design AI-enhanced curricula with teachers, develop AI chatbot mentors and Arduino-based IoT systems, and investigate how these tools shape students' inquiry, reasoning, and data literacy. I also contribute to competitive grant proposals, mentor teachers and graduate students, and serve as a reviewer and organizer for international journals and conferences. Across projects, my goal is to translate cutting-edge AI and educational technology research into practical, responsible, and scalable solutions for classrooms, teacher preparation, and online learning environments.
